Ancient Astronaut Theory

A primer on the idea that otherworldly visitors may have shaped our distant past.

What is it?

The ancient astronaut hypothesis proposes that intelligent extraterrestrial beings visited Earth in antiquity and made contact with humans. The theory suggests that many myths and deities across the world are actually distorted memories of real star‑born visitors.

Origins of the idea

Speculation that life on Earth may have been seeded or guided by extraterrestrial intelligence can be traced back to early science‑fiction works in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. In the 1950s and 60s, writers such as Harold T. Wilkins and Erich von Däniken popularised the notion that ancient monuments like the Egyptian pyramids or the Nazca lines were built with alien help. Contemporary advocates range from independent researchers to TV personalities, each adding their own spin to the narrative.

Frequently asked questions

What does “ancient astronaut” mean?

It refers to the hypothesis that visitors from other star systems reached Earth thousands of years ago and interacted with early human societies, leaving behind physical traces and cultural memories.

Why is mainstream academia so skeptical?

Most archaeologists and historians argue that extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence. They point out that documented techniques, local quarry sources and human ingenuity explain most ancient feats without invoking extraterrestrials.
