Abydos “Helicopter” Hieroglyphs

On a ceiling beam in the Temple of Seti I at Abydos, overlapping hieroglyphs appear to resemble a helicopter, submarine and other modern vehicles. These images have been cited as proof of ancient technology. However, Egyptologists explain that the carvings are palimpsests created when Ramesses II re‑carved his inscription over his father Seti I’s hieroglyphs; over time the plaster filler eroded, exposing both sets of carvings and creating the illusion of modern shapes【501275152705584†L140-L149】.

  • Status: refuted

Why It’s Intriguing

The shapes produced by the overlapping inscriptions do resemble a helicopter and submarine to modern eyes, sparking viral images and speculation about ancient high technology. The temple itself is ancient, lending a veneer of authenticity to the pseudoarchaeological interpretation.

Orthodox Explanation

Scholars note that re-use of stone and overcarving were common in Egypt. When Ramesses II commissioned inscriptions, he chiselled away his father’s text and filled gaps with plaster before carving his own. Over millennia the plaster eroded, leaving fragments of both inscriptions that coincidentally form shapes reminiscent of modern machines【501275152705584†L140-L149】. The shapes are a classic example of pareidolia—seeing patterns where none exist.

Pro‑Theory Reading (Hypothesis)

Ancient astronaut proponents argue that the carvings are intentional depictions of advanced craft—perhaps aircraft or submarines—seen by the ancient Egyptians. They suggest that knowledge of such vehicles could only have come from contact with extraterrestrial visitors. Critics counter that there is no corroborating evidence of advanced technology in the archaeological record.

Contextual Notes

This case hinges on iconographic interpretation rather than measurable metrics. No charts are available.
