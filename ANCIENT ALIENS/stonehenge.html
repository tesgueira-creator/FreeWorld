Stonehenge

Stonehenge is a prehistoric stone circle in Wiltshire, England, built between 3000 and 2000 BC. The monument’s outer ring of standing sarsen stones is around 4 m high and weighs about 25 tonnes each【629210299690092†L320-L325】, while the interior trilithons comprise uprights and lintels weighing up to 50 tonnes【629210299690092†L610-L617】. The structure aligns with the summer solstice sunrise and winter solstice sunset【629210299690092†L320-L329】, hinting at astronomical significance.

Technical Data

  • Date: c. 3000–2000 BC (Neolithic/Bronze Age)
  • Materials: sarsen sandstone and bluestone
  • Mass: Sarsen stones ~25 t each【629210299690092†L320-L325】; trilithon uprights and lintels up to 50 t【629210299690092†L610-L617】
  • Quarry distance: sarsens from 25 km north; bluestones from Preseli Hills (~220 km)
  • Alignment: axis aligns with solstices【629210299690092†L320-L329】
  • Status: accepted

Why It’s Intriguing

Stonehenge’s megaliths are arranged with remarkable precision for a Neolithic society. Transporting 25‑tonne sarsen blocks from 25 km away and 4‑tonne bluestones from Wales required coordinated logistics. The monument’s alignment with the solstices suggests astronomical knowledge and ritual purpose. The sophistication of engineering and alignment fosters speculation about lost technological skills or guidance.

Orthodox Explanation

Most archaeologists view Stonehenge as a prehistoric ceremonial site constructed using wooden sledges, ropes, and human labour. The stones were transported via drag paths and waterways, then erected using timber frames and ramps. Astronomical alignments may have served calendrical functions or marked seasonal rituals. The monument evolved over centuries, reflecting changing social and religious practices rather than a single sudden construction.

Pro‑Theory Reading (Hypothesis)

Ancient astronaut theorists focus on the massive weight of Stonehenge’s lintels and the precision of its astronomical alignment. They question how Neolithic people, lacking the wheel or metal tools, transported and lifted the stones. The involvement of visitors with advanced knowledge is proposed to explain both the engineering and the cosmological design, suggesting Stonehenge served as an observatory or beacon.
