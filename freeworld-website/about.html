About FreeWorld XenoData

Our mission, principles and the people behind the research.

Mission

FreeWorld XenoData is a local‑first investigative lab dedicated to understanding unidentified aerial phenomena through rigorous analysis. We believe that transparent science, robust methodology and open communication can demystify the unknown. By keeping all data processing offline, we ensure privacy and control over sensitive information.

Constraints

We adhere to a strict local‑only policy: no external APIs, trackers or data scraping. All analytic routines run in the browser or in build‑time workers. This protects user privacy and ensures reproducibility. Our datasets are intentionally limited to those provided in the project—sightings.csv and points_of_interest.public.en. Additional sources will require explicit integration and documentation.

Roadmap

Future versions of FreeWorld XenoData will expand the analytics lab to include predictive models, incorporate more nuanced natural language processing for text analysis, and enrich the National Parks section with environmental overlays. We also plan to add a narrative builder so investigators can assemble storylines from charts, maps and notes into structured reports.

Credits

This project synthesises ideas from open‑source developers, UX designers and data scientists. We thank the global UFO research community for inspiring this work. All code and assets are developed within this environment; no external contributions are pulled without explicit attribution.
