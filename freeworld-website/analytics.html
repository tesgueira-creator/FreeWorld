Analytics Lab

Visualise patterns, trends and hidden structures in the sightings data.

Sighting Volume

This chart plots the number of sightings per shape each month. Spikes point to bursts of activity, but caution: z‑scores assume stationarity and cannot distinguish between genuine flurries and reporting artefacts.

Limitations: dataset may be biased toward certain regions; smoothing techniques are not applied here.

Shape Evolution

Observe how the prevalence of discs, spheres and other shapes changes over time. Plateaus could indicate persistent phenomena; declines might reflect fading interest or classification changes.

Limitations: shapes are normalised at load time; rare categories may be underrepresented.

Confidence Trend

Tracking mean confidence scores across months reveals how sure witnesses feel about what they saw. Upswings may correspond to clear conditions or improvements in observation equipment.

Limitations: confidence is subjective and self‑reported; extreme values may distort the mean.

City Persistence

Cities with consistent monthly reports stand out as persistent clusters. Such persistence might indicate long‑term surveillance or simply well‑connected local communities reporting more often.

Limitations: this view does not control for population size or reporting platforms.

Shape Diversity

Diversity metrics measure how evenly different shapes are represented. High values reflect a rich mixture of forms, while low values suggest domination by a few types.

Limitations: diversity indices do not capture underlying causes; outliers may skew perception.

Emergence of New Cities

The appearance of new reporting locations over time can highlight expanding awareness or novel flight paths. A surge may coincide with media campaigns or scientific outreach.

Limitations: our data only captures first occurrences; follow‑up reports are not shown here.
